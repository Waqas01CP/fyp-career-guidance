## SECTION 4 — FINAL KCIS SUB-SCALE LIST
*(22 sub-scales covering all degree categories above)*

### Within Investigative (5 sub-scales):
1. **Mathematical/Computational** — CS, AI, Data Science, Mathematics, Statistics, Actuarial, Financial Maths
2. **Biological/Life Sciences** — Biology, Biotechnology, Biochemistry, Genetics, Microbiology, Zoology, Botany
3. **Clinical/Health Sciences** — Medicine, Pharmacy, Nursing, Allied Health *(overlaps Social)*
4. **Physical Sciences** — Physics, Chemistry, Engineering Sciences, Petroleum
5. **Research/Analytical** — Pure Sciences research orientation, postgraduate-track

### Within Realistic (3 sub-scales):
6. **Mechanical/Electrical/Electronic Systems** — ME, EE, Automotive, Industrial, Mechatronics
7. **Civil/Construction/Spatial** — Civil Engineering, structural aspects of Architecture
8. **Technology/Digital Systems** — Computer Systems, Telecom, Embedded, IT Infrastructure

### Within Artistic (5 sub-scales):
9. **Visual/Graphic Design** — Fine Arts, Communication Design, Digital Media
10. **Spatial/Interior/Fashion Design** — Interior Design, Textile Design, Fashion Design
11. **Architecture (design orientation)** — Architecture (aesthetic, form-making aspects)

## SECTION 5 — KCIS QUESTION DESIGN NOTES

### Questions per sub-scale: 4
### Total KCIS questions: 24 × 4 = **96 questions**
*(22 RIASEC-typed sub-scales + 2 cross-cutting sub-scales)*

> Note: Reference v2 Section 6.2 states 88 questions (22 × 4). That count excluded the
> two cross-cutting sub-scales. The correct total is 96. Reference v2 Section 6.12
> question count (249 total) should be updated accordingly: 249 + 8 = **257 questions**.
> Fazal should write all 24 × 4 = 96 KCIS questions.

### Question writing principles:
1. Each question must discriminate between *adjacent* sub-scales on the hexagon
2. A good Mathematical/Computational question must NOT be answerable by someone with only Physical Sciences interest
3. Questions must refer to *activities*, not to degree names (students may not know degree options)
4. Questions must be answerable by a 16-18 year old with no work experience
5. Both English and Roman Urdu versions required (consistent with RIASEC quiz pattern)

### Example discrimination pairs requiring careful question design:
- Mathematical/Computational vs Physical Sciences (both Investigative — key discriminator: *formulas and algorithms* vs *experiments and physical phenomena*)
- Clinical/Patient Care (Social) vs Biological/Life Sciences (Investigative) — key discriminator: *working with patients* vs *working with specimens/data*)
- Business/Commerce vs Financial/Accounting — key discriminator: *leading teams and strategy* vs *precision, data, structured systems*)
- Media/Communication vs Visual/Graphic Design — key discriminator: *telling stories and persuading* vs *creating visual form and aesthetics*)

## SECTION 7 — PROGRAMMES REQUIRING SPECIAL HANDLING

### Programmes outside the 15-18 age target but in the system:
- MBBS, BDS — extremely competitive, 5 years, MDCAT required. FilterNode merit cutoff enforcement is the primary value-add for these.
- LLB (5-year) — academic law track, not commonly pursued from school directly in Karachi but HEC-recognised.
- Pharm-D — 5 years, pre-medical requirement. Sits between Medicine and Pharmacy.

### Programmes with low social acceptability but legitimate matching:
- BS Social Work — Low prestige tier in Karachi context. ExplanationNode must frame carefully.
- BS Visual Studies / Fine Arts — Cultural stigma for many families. KCIS sub-scale (Visual/Graphic Design) still important for identifying students who genuinely belong in creative fields.
- BS Health, Physical Education & Sports Sciences — Not on the prestige ladder but growing with cricket and sports culture.
- BS Religious Studies tracks — Niche market, important for specific student populations.

### Programmes new to Pakistan's degree landscape (high KCIS need):
- BS Artificial Intelligence — Distinct from CS despite overlap. KCIS must differentiate.
- BS Cybersecurity — Growing field, distinct motivational profile from general CS.
- BS Data Science — Analytics/data orientation distinct from algorithmic CS.
- BS Social Development & Policy (Habib) — Unique in Karachi, no equivalent elsewhere.
